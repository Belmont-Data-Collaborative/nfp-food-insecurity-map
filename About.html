About the data behind this map.

Every layer in this tool is sourced from public data or NFP operational records. This page documents each source, its vintage, known caveats, and the transformations we applied to get it onto the map. Read this before citing a number in a grant proposal or a board meeting.

The map harmonizes five datasets at two geographic grains — 2020 Census tracts (434 tracts) and 2020 ZCTAs — clipped to the 14-county Nashville MSA.

Processing pipeline

Raw sources land in /data/raw. They pass through a four-step pipeline before the map sees them.

Step 01
Ingest
Fetch from APIs or scraped registry; normalize column names; tag with vintage.
Step 02
Harmonize
Crosswalk geographies to 2020 tracts; uppercase GEOIDs to 11 digits.
Step 03
Classify
LLM categorizes nonprofit mission statements into 10 service categories.
Step 04
Publish
Write slim GeoJSON + indicator CSVs to /public/data.

USDA LILA 2010 → 2020 crosswalk

USDA's Food Access Research Atlas is still reported on 2010 census tracts, while every other dataset in this tool is on 2020 tracts. A direct join loses ~18% of tract-level LILA flags.

Our approach

  • Start with the Census 2010→2020 tract relationship file (TIGER).
  • Where a 2010 tract maps 1:1 to a 2020 tract, copy flags directly.
  • Where a 2010 tract splits into multiple 2020 tracts, propagate the LILA flag to all children.
  • Where multiple 2010 tracts merge into one 2020 tract, apply the flag if any parent was LILA (conservative — favors identifying need).
  • Population variables (lapop1, lalowi1) are apportioned by area-weighted overlap.
Caveat. This crosswalk introduces 3–6% error at the tract level. For county-level aggregates the error is negligible. For tract-level planning, treat LILA flags as indicative, not definitive.

Partner classification rubric

The 10 categories used for Giving Matters nonprofit classification:

CategoryDefinition
school_summerPrograms serving K–12 students during school year or summer break
medical_healthClinics, pharmacies, health screenings, mental-health services
transitional_housingShort-term housing for people transitioning out of crisis
senior_servicesPrograms targeting adults 55+
community_developmentNeighborhood revitalization, economic development, community-building
homeless_outreachDirect services for people experiencing homelessness
workforce_developmentJob training, career counseling, skills education
after_schoolOut-of-school-time programs for children
community_mealsMeal programs, food pantries, community kitchens
otherOrgs that don't fit the above or ambiguous mission text
Evaluation. 150 randomly-sampled classifications were human-reviewed in March 2026. Agreement with ground truth: 91.3%. The most confused pair is community_meals ↔ homeless_outreach, which often overlap in practice.

Known caveats

  • Vintage mismatch. USDA LILA is from 2019. ACS is 2020–24. CDC PLACES is 2024. We show the most recent available but readers should know they don't all describe the same year.
  • Modeled vs measured. PLACES diabetes rates are modeled, not observed. Treat as a directional signal, not a count of cases.
  • Suppressed tracts. ACS suppresses estimates for tracts with very small populations. These appear as "—" on the map.
  • LILA boundary error. 3–6% tract-level error from the 2010→2020 crosswalk. See above.
  • Partner registry is a snapshot. Giving Matters changes weekly; we refresh quarterly. Some listed orgs may have closed or merged.
  • Not a census of food assistance. Not every food-providing org is in Giving Matters. Informal, church-based, and mutual-aid networks are under-represented.
